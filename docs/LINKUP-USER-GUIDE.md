# Your LinkUp guide

*Everything here is written for real people—not engineers. Short sentences. No jargon. You’ve got this.*

---

## What is LinkUp?

**LinkUp helps you discover meetups near you, chat with the person hosting, agree on the details, and—when money is involved—pay in a safer, structured way.**

Think of it like this:

- You **browse plans** people have posted (coffee, fitness, events, paid experiences—whatever fits the community). You can swipe through ideas like a dating app, or switch to a **list** if you prefer.
- You **save** plans you love (with **Premium**), **send offers**, and **negotiate** time, place, and price when it’s a fit.
- You **agree on paper** in the app—and for **paid meetups**, money can sit in a **holding step** until things go as agreed. That’s **escrow**: funds are set aside through a secure checkout, then released when the meetup side of the deal is honored (or a support path is used).
- **Premium** adds perks (boosts, deeper filters, travel browsing, save, undo, and seeing who’s interested in *your* plans). **Premium is not a substitute for identity verification.** Hosting paid plans, sending offers, and funding escrow still require you to be verified when the app says so.

LinkUp is built for **real-world meetups** and **trust**. That’s why some steps ask you to verify who you are before you host, negotiate money, or pay through the app.

## How the app is laid out

After you’re signed in and finished onboarding, your home is the **bottom tab bar**. You’ll see **five** areas (icons, no tiny labels—here’s what they mean):

| Tab | What it’s for |
|-----|----------------|
| **Discover** (heart) | **Nearby plans**—swipe or list, search, filters, and the **+** button to **create** a plan. |
| **Messages** | **Chats**—one-to-one conversations with people you’re talking to. |
| **Saved** (bookmark) | Plans you’ve **bookmarked** (**Premium**). |
| **Offers** (tag) | **Your negotiations**—offers you’ve **sent** and offers you’ve **received**, in one place. |
| **Profile** | **You**—completion, verification, stats, Premium, and **settings**. |

You’ll usually land on **Discover** first. That’s on purpose: it’s the “what’s happening around me?” screen.

## Getting started (first time)

### 1. Sign in

You can sign in with **email and password** or **Google** (whichever the login screen offers). If something fails, double-check your connection and try again—no shame in a second attempt.

### 2. Build your profile (onboarding)

Onboarding is a short **wizard**—about **five steps**—with things like:

- **Photos** and **basics** (name, birthday, a light “adult” confirmation where required)
- **Hinge-style prompts** and **interest tags** so your personality shows
- **Who you’d like to meet** and **how far** you’re willing to browse (**radius**)
- **Languages** and small **preference** toggles
- A **safety** moment (clear tips—not a lecture)
- A **preview** of how your card might look to others

You can **leave and come back**. The app remembers **where you left off** when onboarding is still in progress.

**Important:** Finishing onboarding is **not** the same as **identity verification**. You can enter the main app and look around after onboarding. Verification kicks in when you try trust-heavy actions (see below).

### 3. Location (optional but helpful)

Discover works best when the app knows **roughly where you are**. You’ll see prompts to allow location—used for **nearby** plans and distances. You can decline, but “near you” will be fuzzier.

**Premium:** If you subscribe, **Travel mode** (under **Profile → Travel mode**) lets you **browse another city** before you arrive—handy for trips.

## Discover: browsing plans

On **Discover** you can:

- **Swipe** through cards (pass / show interest—like the familiar dating-app motion), **or** switch to a **list** view if you like scanning faster.
- **Search** by keywords when you know what you want.
- Open **filters**: distance, price cap, and (where available) **verified hosts only**. Some filter depth is tied to **Premium**—the app will tell you.
- Tap **+** to **create** a plan (verified hosts—see below).

**You do not need to be verified** to scroll, search, and open plans. LinkUp wants you to see what’s out there first.

If you hide a plan by mistake, **Premium** may offer an **undo** so you can bring the last one back.

## Saved plans (Premium)

The **Saved** tab lists plans you’ve **bookmarked**. Saving is a **Premium** feature—if you’re not subscribed, the app will steer you to **Premium** when you try to save.

Use it as a “come back later” shelf for ideas you liked while swiping or browsing.

## Offers tab

The **Offers** tab is your **negotiation dashboard**:

- Flip between **Sent** and **Received**.
- See what’s **pending**, **accepted**, or **stale**, and jump back to the **plan** when you need context.

It’s the bird’s-eye view so you don’t lose a conversation across many screens.

## Identity verification—why it exists

Some actions are sensitive: **publishing a plan**, **sending offers**, **accepting** in a way that locks terms, and **moving money** in escrow. To reduce scams and keep meetups safer, LinkUp asks you to **verify your identity** before those actions.

**You might see:**

- A gentle **gate** on create, offer, or payment—with a path to **Start verification** or **Resume** if you already began.
- Banners or hints on **Discover** when you’re **unverified** (you can still browse).

**Verification is about trust—not about showing your ID on your public profile.** Your documents confirm you’re real; they don’t decorate your card.

### What the verification flow feels like

When you start (from **Profile → Verification status** or the full **KYC** flow the app opens), you’ll move through simple steps—typically:

1. **Trust intro** — why LinkUp does this and what it unlocks.  
2. **Choose your ID type** — e.g. national ID, passport, driver’s license, or voter card.  
3. **Photo of your ID** — clear, no glare; you can often **retake** or **upload** from your gallery.  
4. **Short selfie video** — quick liveness check (usually a few seconds).  
5. **Consent** — you agree to how verification data is used.  
6. **Review** — your submission is **in progress**; you’ll get an outcome (approved or needs another try).

You can often **save progress** and **come back**. If you’re **already waiting on a decision**, you should see that—you’re not “starting over” from zero; you can **resume** or **check status**.

## Creating a plan (verified hosts)

Want to host? On **Discover**, tap **+** (floating button).

**Step 1 — Details**

- **Title** and optional **story**
- **Where** (location picker / label)
- **When** (date and time)
- Optional **starting price** (app uses **NGN** in the current product)

**Step 2 — Who can see it**

You choose **visibility**:

- **Public** — anyone on LinkUp can discover it in the feed.  
- **Within radius** — geared to people roughly within your discovery radius.  
- **Friends only** — tighter audience (as connections features evolve, this may get stricter automatically).

Then you **publish**. You’ll land on a **success** moment and can open your new plan.

**If you’re not verified yet**, the app will block publish and offer **verification**—that’s normal, not a bug.

## Plan screen (open a meetup)

When you open a plan you’ll see **details**, **host** info, **price** (if any), and actions such as:

- **Message** / **Chat** with the other person (opens your **Messages** thread).
- **Negotiate** (when the plan is in negotiation and you’re not the host)—see below.
- **Add to calendar** (when the app allows it).
- **Save** (**Premium**) and **Boost** (**Premium**) for hosts who want more visibility.
- **Interest** (**Premium**, **host only**)—see who **viewed** or **saved** your plan.

Tap **profiles** to learn more about someone when the app links to their card.

---

## Negotiation: “Drop your idea”

When a plan is **negotiating** and you’re a **guest** (not the host), you’ll open the **negotiation** screen. Think of it in two layers:

1. **Plan the meetup** — a card at the top that explains how rounds work and nudges you to **Open chat** for the human stuff.  
2. **Drop your idea** — a **bottom sheet** you can **drag up or down** (like maps or music apps): **collapsed** (quick peek), **mid**, or **almost full screen**—still **below** the meetup card so you don’t lose context.

Inside the sheet you can:

- Use **quick sparks** (time suggestions, light lines you can add to your note).
- Set **optional paid amount** (“skip for now” is fine).
- Pick **when** with the date/time control.
- Add a **note** and **send** your suggestion.

**Offers expire** after a set time (the UI says it in friendly copy—treat it like a soft deadline). There’s a **maximum number of offer rounds** on a single plan so things don’t drag forever—use **chat** for the warm, messy details.

**Host** sees offers in the same timeline and can **accept** or **decline** when something lands.

**Verification:** Guests need to be **verified** to **send** offers; hosts need to be **verified** to **accept** in product flows that require it.

## Agreeing on the meetup

When the **host accepts** an offer, you’ll open the **agreement** screen—a **structured summary**:

- **Who** and **what** you agreed  
- **When** / **where** (or the clearest text the app has)  
- **Price** (if any)—free vs paid  

From there:

- **Free plans:** confirm you’re aligned and **complete** the flow the app shows.  
- **Paid plans:** the guest may **continue to secure payment**—that creates or opens an **escrow** journey.

Read it like a confirmation before a trip. If something’s wrong, **don’t tap through**—**message** the other person or step back until it’s clear.

---

## Escrow and payments (simple version)

For **paid** plans after agreement, LinkUp uses a **structured payment path** (in the current app this goes through **Paystack** checkout in **NGN**).

**In plain words:**

- The **payer** completes checkout in a **secure** web flow the app opens.  
- Funds move into **escrow**—**held** until the steps you both agreed to are honored.  
- The app shows **status**, a **timeline**, and buttons for things like **mark funded**, **release**, or **confirm the meetup**—wording depends on where you are in the flow.  
- If something goes wrong, you can start a **dispute** from the escrow screen so support can review.

**You generally need to be verified** to **fund** or complete sensitive money steps—that protects everyone.

## Disputes

**Profile → Disputes** lists **escrow-linked disputes** tied to you. If you opened one from escrow, you’ll see it here with a **status**.

**Admins** resolve disputes in internal tools; your job in-app is to **describe honestly** and **respond** if support reaches out.

## Premium

**Profile** surfaces a **Premium** card; you can also reach **Premium** from upsells in the app.

**What Premium tends to include** (exact copy may vary slightly in-app):

- **Boost** plans so they surface higher in Discover.  
- **See who’s interested**—views and saves on **your** plans (opens from plan actions).  
- **Advanced filters** (price, distance, verified hosts).  
- **Travel mode**—browse another city.  
- **Saved** tab and **undo** for the last hidden plan.

**Again:** paying for Premium **does not** skip **identity verification** for hosting, offers, or escrow. The Premium screen says this clearly on purpose.

## Messages and chat

**Messages** lists your **one-to-one** threads. You might open a chat from a **plan**, **negotiation**, **escrow**, or the inbox—**same conversation**, different doors in.

**Messaging** is usually available **before** you’re fully verified, but spam controls and limits can apply for brand-new accounts. **Hosting, offering, and paying** are stricter—that’s by design.

## Notification inbox

**Profile → Notification inbox** (or the entry point the bell / badge uses) is your **in-app notification center**:

- **Today** vs **earlier** sections  
- Filters for types like **meetups**, **escrow**, **verification**  
- Swipe or actions to **read**, **clear**, or **remove** items  
- Tapping an item can **deep-link** you to the right screen (plan, escrow, etc.)

This is separate from **phone** notification **settings**—those live under **Notifications & visibility**.

## Your profile and account

In **Profile** you can:

- See **profile completion** and **verification** at a glance.  
- View **prompts** and tags you set during onboarding.  
- See light **stats** (e.g. meetups shared, completed).  
- Open **Premium** status and upgrades.  
- Dive into **Settings & account** (see below).  
- **Log out** or **Delete account** (with confirmations).  
- **Refresh profile data** if things look stale.

**Admins** may see an **Admin dashboard** link—most accounts won’t have this.

## Staying safe (the human stuff)

- **Meet in public** first when you don’t know someone well.  
- **Share plans** with a friend: who, where, when.  
- **Keep chat in the app** when you can—helps if you ever need support.  
- If someone **pressures** you to pay **outside** the app or skip escrow, that’s a **red flag**.  
- Use **report**, **block**, or **support** tools when the app offers them—and **Help & support** for anything serious.

LinkUp can’t promise every person is perfect—but **verification**, **structured payments**, **clear agreements**, and **dispute paths** exist to **stack the odds** in favor of safer, clearer meetups.

---

## Help when something breaks

If the app errors, payment confuses you, or you feel unsafe:

- Open **Help & support** from **Profile**.  
- Pick a **topic**, read the quick cards, and **open a ticket** if you need a human.  
- For **escrow** issues, use the **dispute** path from the escrow screen when something’s genuinely wrong.

You’re not bothering anyone—that’s what Support is for.
