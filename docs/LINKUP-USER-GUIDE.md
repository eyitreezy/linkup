# Your LinkUp guide

*Everything here is written for real people—not engineers. Short sentences. No jargon. You've got this.*

---

## What is LinkUp?

**LinkUp helps you discover meetups near you, chat with the person hosting, agree on the details, and—when money is involved—pay in a safer, structured way.**

Think of it like this:

- You **browse plans** people have posted (coffee, fitness, events, mood moments, paid experiences—whatever fits the community). **Swipe** through ideas like a dating app, or switch to a **list** from the filter sheet.
- You **save** plans you love (**Premium**), track **offers** you've sent and received, and **negotiate** time, place, and price when it's a fit.
- You **agree in the app**—and for **paid meetups**, money can sit in a **holding step** until things go as agreed. That's **escrow**: funds are set aside through secure checkout (**Paystack**, **NGN**), then released when the meetup side of the deal is honored (or a support path is used).
- **Premium** adds perks (boosts, deeper filters, travel browsing, save, undo, and seeing who's interested in *your* plans). **Premium is not a substitute for identity verification.** Hosting paid plans, sending offers, and funding escrow still require verification when the app says so.

LinkUp is built for **real-world meetups** and **trust**. That's why some steps ask you to verify who you are before you host, negotiate money, or pay through the app.

## Opening the app

When you cold-start LinkUp, you'll see a **branded splash screen** for about **five seconds**:

- LinkUp **logo** and wordmark on a purple-to-pink gradient
- Tagline: **"Discover meetups near you."** and **"Meet with confidence."**
- A smooth fade into the app once your session loads

This replaces the old generic loading spinner—you shouldn't see a bare spinner on launch anymore.

## How the app is laid out

After you're signed in and finished onboarding, your home is the **bottom tab bar**. You'll see **six** areas (icons, no tiny labels—here's what they mean):

| Tab | What it's for |
|-----|----------------|
| **Discover** (heart) | **Nearby meetups**—swipe cards (default), mood timeline, filters, and **+** to **create** a plan. |
| **Messages** | **Chats**—inbox plus a strip of people you're actively talking to about plans. |
| **Saved** (bookmark) | Plans you've **bookmarked** (**Premium**). |

| **Profile** | **You**—completion, verification, stats, Premium, and **settings**. |

You'll usually land on **Discover** first. That's on purpose: it's the "what's happening around me?" screen.

## Getting started (first time)

### 1. Sign in

You can sign in with **email and password** or **Google** (whichever the login screen offers). Forgot password? Use the reset flow—the email link opens LinkUp so you can set a new password.

### 2. Build your profile (onboarding)

Onboarding is a short **five-step wizard** with progress saved if you leave:

| Step | What you do |
|------|-------------|
| **1 — Basics** | Display name, birthday, confirm you're **18+**, add **photos** (tap one to set **Primary**), and record a **short intro video** (required). |
| **2 — Your story** | Bio (150 chars max), **interests**, **languages**, **meeting intent** (friendship, dating, activity partner, or networking), and **1–2 prompt answers** (Hinge-style). |
| **3 — Location & prefs** | **Your location** (search or use current location), gender, who you want to see, **age range**, **distance radius**, and whether your profile is **visible**. |
| **4 — Safety** | Read safety tips and acknowledge them. Optional contacts import is informational only in this build. |
| **5 — Preview** | See a **discovery-style preview card** of how others might see you—then continue into the app. |

You can **leave and come back**. The app remembers **which step you were on** while onboarding is still in progress.

**Important:** Finishing onboarding is **not** the same as **identity verification**. You can enter the main app and look around after onboarding. Verification kicks in when you try trust-heavy actions (see below).

### 3. Location (optional but helpful)

Discover works best when the app knows **roughly where you are**. You'll see prompts to allow location—used for **nearby** plans and distances. You can decline, but "near you" will be fuzzier.

**Premium:** **Travel mode** (Profile → Travel mode) lets you **browse another city** before you arrive—the Discover header shows your travel label.

## Discover: browsing meetups

Discover is **swipe-first** by default.

### Swipe mode (default)

- **Tall photo-forward cards** fill the screen above the action buttons.
- **Pass** (X), **Info** (details), and **Like** (heart) buttons sit **directly below** the card—Tinder-style.
- Swipe the card or use the buttons.
- Tap the card or **Info** to open the full **plan screen**.
- Tap the host's photo context to open their **member profile**.

### Mood timeline

Above the main deck, you may see a **horizontal row of mood pills**—short-lived "vibe" meetups (e.g. coffee now, gym later). Tap one to focus the feed on that vibe.

### List mode

Prefer scanning faster? Open **Filters** and switch **Display** to **List**. You'll get scrollable rows and a **search bar** for keywords.

### Filters (filter sheet)

Tap the **filter** control in the header to open the sheet:

| Filter | Notes |
|--------|--------|
| **Display** | **Swipe** or **List** (horizontal pills) |
| **Mood vibe** | All, chill, adventure, etc. |
| **Distance** | Max km slider |
| **Price** | Min / max (Premium depth) |
| **Verified hosts only** | Premium |
| **Host presence** | All / online now / offline |

Your choices persist across sessions.

### Other Discover actions

- **+ Create** (floating button) — host a new meetup (**verify first**).
- **Undo** (Premium) — bring back the last plan you hid from the feed.
- **KYC banner** — gentle reminder if you're unverified (you can still browse).

**You do not need to be verified** to scroll, search, and open plans.

## Identity verification—why it exists

Some actions are sensitive: **publishing a plan**, **sending offers**, **accepting** in a way that locks terms, and **moving money** in escrow. To reduce scams and keep meetups safer, LinkUp asks you to **verify your identity** before those actions.

**You might see:**

- A gentle **gate** on create, offer, or payment—with a path to **Start verification** or **Resume** if you already began.
- Banners or hints on **Discover** when you're **unverified** (you can still browse).

**Verification is about trust—not about showing your ID on your public profile.** Your documents confirm you're real; they don't decorate your card.

### What the verification flow feels like

When you start (from **Profile → Verification status** or the full **KYC** flow):

1. **Trust intro** — why LinkUp does this and what it unlocks.
2. **Choose your ID type** — national ID, passport, driver's license, or voter card.
3. **Photo of your ID** — clear, no glare; retake or gallery upload.
4. **Short selfie video** — quick liveness check (a few seconds).
5. **Consent** — you agree to how verification data is used.
6. **Review** — submission **in progress**; approved or needs another try.

You can **save progress** and **come back**.

## Creating a plan (verified hosts)

Want to host? On **Discover**, tap **+**.

The create flow is a **wizard**:

### Step 1 — Meet & schedule

- Pick a **meet type** (coffee, dinner, gym, mood moment, etc.)
- Set **date & time** and **duration**
- For **mood plans**: choose vibe type and how long the listing stays live

### Step 2 — Commitment

- **Free** or **paid**
- Starting price and escrow pattern hints if paid

### Step 3 — Details

- **Title** and optional **story**
- **Where** (location picker)
- **Visibility**: **Public**, **within radius**, or **friends only**

### Step 4 — Success

- Confirmation and shortcut to your new plan

**If you're not verified yet**, the app blocks publish and offers **verification**—that's normal.

**Hosts with published plans** can open **Profile → Plan management** to see meetups, mood shelf, and drafts.

## Plan screen (open a meetup)

When you open a plan you'll see **details**, **host** info, **price** (if any), and actions such as:

- **Message** / **Chat** with the other person.
- **Negotiate** (when the plan is in negotiation and you're not the host).
- **Add to calendar** (when available).
- **Save** (**Premium**) and **Boost** (**Premium**) for hosts who want more visibility.
- **Interest** (**Premium**, **host only**)—see who **viewed** or **saved** your plan.

Tap **profiles** to open the member screen.

---

## Negotiation: "Drop your idea"

When a plan is **negotiating** and you're a **guest** (not the host), you'll open the **negotiation** screen:

1. **Plan the meetup** — context card; **Open chat** for the human stuff.
2. **Drop your idea** — a **bottom sheet** you can drag (collapsed / mid / expanded):

- **Quick sparks** (time suggestions, conversation starters)
- **Optional paid amount**
- **When** (date/time)
- **Note** and **send**

**Offers expire** after a set time. There's a **maximum number of rounds** per plan—use **chat** for softer details.

**Host** sees offers in the timeline and can **accept** or **decline**.

**Verification:** Guests need to be **verified** to **send** offers; hosts need verification for gated accept flows.

---

## Agreeing on the meetup

When the **host accepts** an offer, you'll open the **agreement** screen:

- **Who** and **what** you agreed
- **When** / **where**
- **Price** (if any)

From there:

- **Free plans:** confirm and complete.
- **Paid plans:** guest may **continue to secure payment** → **escrow**.

Read it like a confirmation before a trip. If something's wrong, **message** the other person first.

---

## Escrow and payments (simple version)

For **paid** plans after agreement, LinkUp uses **Paystack** checkout in **NGN**.

- The **payer** completes checkout in a secure web flow.
- Funds move into **escrow**—held until agreed steps are honored.
- The app shows **status**, a **timeline**, and actions like **mark funded**, **release**, or **confirm the meetup**.
- **Dispute** from the escrow screen if something's wrong.

**You generally need to be verified** to fund or complete sensitive money steps.

## Messages and chat

**Messages** lists your **one-to-one** threads plus an **engagement strip** at the top (people you're actively coordinating with on plans).

You might open a chat from a **plan**, **negotiation**, **escrow**, **member profile**, or the inbox—**same conversation**, different doors in.

In a thread you can:

- Send **text**
- Attach **photos** and **videos**
- **Edit** or **delete** your own messages (where supported)
- See **presence** (online / last seen) when visibility allows

**Messaging** is usually available **before** you're fully verified, but limits may apply for new accounts. **Hosting, offering, and paying** are stricter—that's by design.

## Staying safe (the human stuff)

- **Meet in public** first when you don't know someone well.
- **Share plans** with a friend: who, where, when.
- **Keep chat in the app** when you can—helps if you ever need support.
- If someone **pressures** you to pay **outside** the app or skip escrow, that's a **red flag**.
- Use **report**, **block**, or **support** when offered.

LinkUp can't promise every person is perfect—but **verification**, **structured payments**, **clear agreements**, and **dispute paths** exist to **stack the odds** in favor of safer, clearer meetups.

---

## Help when something breaks

- **Profile → Help & support** — topics, quick cards, **open a ticket**.
- **Escrow disputes** — from the escrow screen when something's genuinely wrong.
- **Profile → Disputes** — track status.

You're not bothering anyone—that's what Support is for.
